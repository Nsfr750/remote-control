# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all community members with respect and dignity
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and encourage participation from all backgrounds
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful, constructive feedback
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together to solve problems and improve the project
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional conduct in all interactions
- **Be Patient**: Understand that people have different levels of experience and knowledge

### Specific Guidelines

#### Communication
- **Use inclusive language** and avoid offensive terms
- **Listen actively** to different perspectives
- **Assume good intent** unless proven otherwise
- **Provide constructive criticism** that helps improve the project
- **Acknowledge and learn from mistakes**

#### Technical Discussions
- **Focus on technical merits** of ideas and solutions
- **Back up claims** with evidence and reasoning
- **Be open to alternative approaches** and solutions
- **Admit when you're wrong** or don't know something
- **Give credit** where credit is due

#### Code Reviews
- **Be thorough but fair** in your reviews
- **Explain your reasoning** clearly and politely
- **Suggest improvements** rather than just pointing out problems
- **Thank contributors** for their work and effort
- **Help new contributors** learn and improve

### Unacceptable Behavior

#### Harassment
- **Personal attacks** or insults
- **Trolling** or deliberate provocation
- **Threats** of any kind
- **Unwelcome sexual advances** or comments
- **Publishing private information** without consent

#### Discrimination
- **Discriminatory language** or actions
- **Stereotyping** based on personal characteristics
- **Exclusionary behavior** that marginalizes groups
- **Microaggressions** or subtle discrimination
- **Cultural insensitivity** or appropriation

#### Disruptive Behavior
- **Spam** or repetitive off-topic content
- **Derailing conversations** from productive discussion
- **Intentionally spreading misinformation**
- **Abusing the reporting system** with false claims
- **Creating multiple accounts** to evade moderation

#### Professional Conduct
- **Plagiarism** or claiming others' work as your own
- **Sabotaging** others' work or contributions
- **Misusing project resources** for personal gain
- **Violating licenses** or intellectual property rights
- **Engaging in illegal activities**

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

#### For Immediate Issues
- **Email**: [info@tuxxle.org](mailto:info@tuxxle.org)
- **Subject**: [CONDUCT] Issue Description
- **Response Time**: Within 48 hours

#### For GitHub Issues
- **Use "conduct" label** for conduct-related issues
- **Provide specific details** about the incident
- **Include screenshots** or links when applicable
- **Maintain privacy** by reporting privately when appropriate

### What to Include

- **Description**: What happened, when, and where
- **People Involved**: Who was affected (if known)
- **Impact**: How it affected the community or individuals
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, links, or other documentation
- **Desired Resolution**: What outcome you're seeking

### Confidentiality

- **Privacy**: All reports will be kept confidential
- **Protection**: Reporters will be protected from retaliation
- **Discretion**: Only involved parties will be informed
- **Documentation**: Reports will be documented for pattern tracking

## Enforcement Process

### Investigation

1. **Initial Response**
   - Acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
   - Assess immediate safety concerns
   - Provide temporary measures if needed
   - Set expectations for resolution timeline

2. **Fact-Finding**
   - Gather evidence from all parties
   - Review relevant logs and communications
   - Consult with maintainers and moderators
   - Check for pattern of behavior

3. **Decision Making**
   - Evaluate against code of conduct standards
   - Consider context and intent
   - Ensure fair and consistent application
   - Document reasoning for decisions

### Consequences

#### First Offense
- **Warning**: Private warning with explanation
- **Education**: Resources on appropriate behavior
- **Temporary Restrictions**: 24-48 hour moderation if needed
- **Required Action**: Public apology or content removal

#### Second Offense
- **Temporary Suspension**: 1-2 week ban from community spaces
- **Mandatory Review**: Review of conduct guidelines
- **Supervised Participation**: Limited interaction privileges
- **Formal Warning**: Documentation of offense

#### Serious Offenses
- **Immediate Ban**: Permanent removal from community
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement if needed
- **Platform Ban**: Removal from all project platforms
- **Public Statement**: Communication to community as appropriate

#### Appeals Process
- **Appeal Window**: 7 days from decision
- **Review Committee**: Different maintainers review appeal
- **New Evidence**: Consideration of additional information
- **Final Decision**: Appeal decision is final

## Community Guidelines

### Contributing

#### New Contributors
- **Welcome newcomers** and help them get started
- **Provide guidance** on project processes and standards
- **Be patient** with questions and learning curves
- **Mentorship**: Offer help when you have expertise
- **Recognition**: Acknowledge and appreciate contributions

#### Experienced Contributors
- **Lead by example** in conduct and quality
- **Mentor others** and share knowledge
- **Review constructively** and help improve contributions
- **Document decisions** and share reasoning
- **Maintain standards** and uphold project values

### Conflict Resolution

#### Disagreements
- **Focus on issues**, not personalities
- **Seek common ground** and compromise solutions
- **Take discussions offline** when they become unproductive
- **Involve moderators** when consensus cannot be reached
- **Respect final decisions** even if you disagree

#### Misunderstandings
- **Assume good intent** initially
- **Clarify understanding** before reacting
- **Apologize sincerely** when mistakes are made
- **Learn from experience** and improve communication
- **Move forward** constructively after resolution

## Scope

### Covered Spaces

This code of conduct applies to:

- **GitHub repositories**: Issues, pull requests, discussions
- **Communication channels**: Email, Discord, Slack, forums
- **Community events**: Meetups, conferences, workshops
- **Project documentation**: Comments, reviews, contributions
- **Social media**: Official project accounts and mentions

### Personal Conduct

This code of conduct also applies when:

- **Representing the project**: In public or professional settings
- **Speaking at events**: As a project representative
- **Writing about the project**: In blogs, articles, or publications
- **Interacting with users**: In support or community contexts
- **Collaborating externally**: With other projects or organizations

## Commitment

### Project Leadership

We commit to:

- **Enforce this code** of conduct fairly and consistently
- **Provide training** on community guidelines and standards
- **Respond promptly** to conduct reports and concerns
- **Protect reporters** from retaliation and harassment
- **Review and update** this code regularly

### Community Members

We ask all community members to:

- **Read and understand** this code of conduct
- **Follow these guidelines** in all project interactions
- **Report violations** when they occur
- **Help maintain** a positive and inclusive environment
- **Continuously improve** our community culture
